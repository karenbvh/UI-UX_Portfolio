Objective

A store selling Japanese culinary candies natively known as 和菓子(wagashi).

I want to create a culture experience in my shop and at the same time allowing easy shopping.

Project focus:

  • Easy candy search and shopping.
  • Researching client needs.

Research



Candy markets in United States are anticipating to have an attractive growth on 2018 to 2025 due to the huge demand in consumer goods. On 2016 non-chocolate candies held a significant revenue due to the variety of flavors, forms of candies available and motivational factors from online stores such as discounted price and lucrative offers, thereby, facilitating a hassle-free shopping experience. According to Grandview Research, urbanization is an increasing trend aiding candy market growth because it has led to awaress of candy products.

Candy Market Competitive Landscape

Ferrara Candy Co., Mars Inc., Mondeléz International Inc, Demet’s Candy Co., Arcor, August Storck Kg, Yildiz Holding and Nestlé SA.


Japanese Candy Outlook

According to NCA (National Confectioners Association) The Japanese confectionary candy market is of strongest in the world and it is expected to grow over the course of 2025. What makes Japanese confectionary art candy so popular? Japanese candies boast a colorful variety of flavors representing flavors like pumpkin, soy sauce, musk melon, sake, just to name a few and a variety of shapes like cute characters, elegant shapes, and really quircky designs.

Conclusion

Wagashi candy makerts are not common in United States meaning that my app will need to introduce more visuals to teach my users how to prepare the candies, how to get the best experience and cultural notes.

There is very little research in the US wagashi consumer market making a bit more difficult to design user interaction and adding a few marketing hooks. To solve that problem, I chose to present categories as the main page to guide navigation and interaction during the shopping process.
