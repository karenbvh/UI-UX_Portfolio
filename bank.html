Objective

As the number of financial mobile application keeps increasing and paying with your mobile device is easier and faster, more people fail to plan their long or short term finances; this is not often tie to lack of self control or income level.


The goal is to make an app that makes visually easy on keeping track of your financial goals, by setting up financial spending rules and a graph to display the user's progress.

Research



After looking at the app JOY, I noticed the designers were using dating psychological tricks used in some dating apps. Joy offers a virtual money coach that is programmed to learn the user's spending habits; this is an amazing technolgy.

What I can take from this is that the user can choose or tailor their saving rules. For example, in Acorns app, it encourages the user to invest their spare change by activting 'Round-Ups' by monitoring the bank account and automatically invest the changes from daily purchase.



According to Market Research, it shows that American consumers increased their spending either by using a form of virtual payment or banking cards for household essential, healthcare, education and entertainment more than they did five years ago. Therefore, virtual spending does not allow to anticipate for future spending.

Conclusion

The objective is to save money by creating a personal mobile app that puts the user in control of their saving habits, however, although it does not have any 'fancy' technology, it does have minimal, useful and successful features to keep track of their progress towards achieving their goal and easy withdrawal of funds to avoid any potential frustration.

Allowing the user to witness their personal saving progress will bring self reward and alleviate an uncertain future.
